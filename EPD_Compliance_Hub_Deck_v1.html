Standards Foundation

What is an Environmental Product Declaration?

An EPD is a standardised, independently verified document that quantifies the environmental impact of a product across its full lifecycle — from raw material extraction to end of life. It is governed by ISO 14025 and EN 15804+A2.

ISO 14025:2006 defines the principles and procedures for Type III environmental declarations — independently verified, LCA-based, quantified environmental data per declared unit.

EN 15804+A2 is the core European standard for construction products, defining the lifecycle modules A1 through D and the mandatory environmental indicators every EPD must report.

Why it matters now
EU Construction Products Regulation mandates EPDs for CE-marked products
UK Net Zero Building Standard references EPD data in procurement
Japan Green Building mandates — no EPD means no specification, no bid, no sale
Major clients (Lixil, GROHE, Roca) now require EPDs from all Tier 1 suppliers
Lifecycle modules — EN 15804+A2
A1–A3
Product
Raw material extraction → manufacturing. Supplier-sourced data. Core of every EPD.
A4–A5
Construction
Transport to site (A4) and installation (A5). Project-team sourced. A4 is the hardest module to automate.
B1–B7
Use stage
Maintenance, energy in use, refurbishment. Often modelled from scenarios. Optional for many product categories.
C1–C4
End of life
Deconstruction, waste processing, disposal. Scenario-modelled. Mandatory for EN 15804+A2.
D
Beyond SB
Recycling and recovery potential. Informational — not comparable across EPDs. Scenario-modelled.
Certified outputs
GWP — kg CO₂ eq ODP — kg CFC-11 eq AP — kg SO₂ eq EP — kg PO₄ eq POCP — smog ADP — fossil + mineral

The Problem

Five challenges that break EPD compliance for QMS teams

These are not technology gaps — they are structural data problems that no EPD calculation tool can solve because they all start downstream of where the data actually lives.

📋
CHALLENGE 1

Material data is trapped in silos

Supplier specs, composition certificates, and manufacturing data live in emails, PDFs, and ERPs — never structured or connected to EN 15804-compatible taxonomy. Every EPD starts from scratch.

Padma's pain: "I spend 60% of my time chasing supplier datasheets."
🔗
CHALLENGE 2

A4 transport data doesn't exist in EPD tools

EN 15804 A4 module requires Tier 1–3 supplier origins and transport routes. No EPD software has access to procurement data. It gets estimated or omitted — creating a compliance gap.

Impact: A4 is the most commonly incorrect module in industrial EPDs.
⏱
CHALLENGE 3

EPDs expire and nobody is watching

5-year expiry. Supplier compositions change. Regulatory requirements update. Today there is no system monitoring this continuously — it's a calendar reminder that gets missed in a busy QA function.

Padma's risk: expired EPDs discovered during customer audit, not before.
🔍
CHALLENGE 4

No traceability from source to certificate

When an auditor asks "where did this GWP figure come from?" — there is no answer. Data was assembled manually across disconnected tools with no immutable audit log. Re-declaration means starting over.

ISO 14025 §8 requires independent verification — impossible without lineage.
⚠️
Challenge 5 — The calculation tools solve the wrong problem

SimaPro and OneClick LCA have powerful calculation engines sitting on top of weak, manually assembled data. They solve the maths problem. They cannot solve the data problem — because they have no foothold in industrial procurement or QMS workflows. Octave is already inside both.
